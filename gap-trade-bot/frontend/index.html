How to Use Each Tab

A guide to every section of the platform

Gap-Ups Tab

Purpose: Identify stocks that have opened significantly higher than their previous closing price.

How to Use:
  1. Configure Gap Percentage: Set the minimum gap percentage (default: 25%) to filter stocks
  2. View Gap-Up Stocks: The system automatically scans and displays stocks meeting your criteria
  3. Monitor Real-Time: The list updates automatically every 5 minutes during market hours
  4. Take Action: Use the "Add to Entry Bot" feature to track promising stocks
💡 Pro Tip:

Gap-up stocks often experience increased volatility and trading volume, presenting opportunities for quick profits. However, they also carry higher risk, so always use proper risk management.

Historical Tab

Purpose: Access historical market data and analyze past performance to refine your trading strategies.

How to Use:
  1. Select Date Range: Choose the period you want to analyze
  2. Choose Data Type: Select between gap-up data, trade history, or position history
  3. Apply Filters: Filter by ticker symbol, gap percentage, or other criteria
  4. Export Data: Download data in Excel format for external analysis
💡 Pro Tip:

Use historical data to backtest your strategies and identify patterns that lead to successful trades.

Entry Bot Tab

Purpose: Configure automated entry strategies and monitor stocks for entry opportunities.

How to Use:
  1. Set Entry Parameters: Define symbol, volume requirements, and entry time
  2. Submit Strategy: Click "Submit" to start tracking the stock
  3. Monitor Progress: Watch the tracking status for real-time updates
  4. Automatic Entry: The bot will enter positions when all conditions are met
⚠️ Important:

Always verify your parameters before submitting, as entries cannot be cancelled once conditions are met.

Exit Bot Tab

Purpose: Manage active positions and configure automated exit strategies with profit targets and stop losses.

How to Use:
  1. View Active Positions: See all currently held positions
  2. Set Exit Parameters: Configure profit targets and stop losses
  3. Enable Auto-Exit: Turn on automated exit monitoring
  4. Monitor Performance: Track P&L and exit conditions in real-time
💡 Pro Tip:

Use trailing stop losses to protect profits while allowing for upside potential.

Trade History Tab

Purpose: Review completed trades and analyze your trading performance over time.

How to Use:
  1. Filter by Date: Select specific time periods to analyze
  2. Sort and Filter: Organize trades by ticker, P&L, or trade type
  3. Export Data: Download trade history for external analysis
  4. Analyze Patterns: Identify which strategies work best for you
📊 Analytics:

Use trade history to calculate your win rate, average profit/loss, and identify your most profitable trading patterns.

Positions History Tab

Purpose: Track the lifecycle of positions from entry to exit, including unrealized and realized P&L.

How to Use:
  1. View All Positions: See both open and closed positions
  2. Filter by Status: Separate active positions from closed ones
  3. Track Performance: Monitor unrealized P&L for open positions
  4. Export Data: Download position data for portfolio analysis
📈 Portfolio View:

Use this tab to track your overall trading success and identify areas for improvement.

Stats Tab

Purpose: View comprehensive performance statistics and analytics to track your trading success.

How to Use:
  1. View Performance Metrics: See total P&L, win rate, and total positions
  2. Analyze Daily Performance: Review daily P&L trends with interactive charts
  3. Toggle Chart Types: Switch between bar and line charts
  4. Track Progress: Monitor your trading performance over time
📊 Performance Analytics:

Key indicators include total realized P&L, win rate percentage, and daily performance trends.

Backtest Tab

Purpose: Test your trading strategies against historical data to validate their effectiveness before live trading.

How to Use:
  1. Select Strategy: Choose from predefined strategies (Gap Up, Momentum, Mean Reversion, Breakout)
  2. Set Date Range: Define the historical period for testing
  3. Configure Parameters: Set initial capital, position size, and stop loss
  4. Run Backtest: Execute the strategy against historical data
  5. View Equity Curve: See how your portfolio would have performed over time
🧪 Strategy Validation:

Analyze key metrics like total return, win rate, max drawdown, and Sharpe ratio to optimize your approach before live trading.

AI Chat Tab

Purpose: Get real-time assistance and insights from our AI trading assistant.

How to Use:
  1. Ask Questions: Inquire about market conditions, trading strategies, or platform features
  2. Get Analysis: Request analysis of specific stocks or market trends
  3. Strategy Advice: Get recommendations for entry/exit parameters
  4. Market Insights: Receive real-time market commentary and analysis
🤖 AI Assistant:

Our AI assistant can provide personalized trading insights to enhance your decision-making process.

Quantity: Number of shares to trade
Order Type: MKT for Market orders, LIMIT for Limit orders

How These Statistics Are Calculated

Total P&L: Sum of all realized profits and losses from the positions table SELECT SUM(REALIZED) AS TOTAL_PNL FROM POSITIONS;
Win Rate: Percentage of winning positions (realized > 0) vs losing positions (realized < 0) (SELECT COUNT(*) FROM POSITIONS WHERE REALIZED > 0) / (SELECT COUNT(*) FROM POSITIONS WHERE REALIZED < 0)
Total Positions: Count of all positions in the positions table SELECT COUNT(*) FROM POSITIONS;
