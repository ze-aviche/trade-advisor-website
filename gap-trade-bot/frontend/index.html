Platform Guide

Step-by-step instructions for every feature — updated to match the current version

Gap-Ups Tab

Live scanner

Scans for stocks that have gapped up significantly from the previous session's close. Updates automatically every 5 minutes during market hours. Stocks that appear for the first time since the last refresh flash yellow briefly.

Filters & Controls
  • Gap % filter — slide or type a minimum gap percentage. Lower values (e.g. 5%) show more stocks; higher values (e.g. 25%) show only large movers.
  • Date picker (calendar) — by default shows today's scan. Click the calendar icon to browse any past date that has a saved snapshot.
  • Premarket / Regular tabs — switch between stocks that gapped before the open and those trading in the regular session.
Understanding the Columns
  • Ticker — stock symbol. Sticky on the left so it stays visible when scrolling right on mobile.
  • Gap % — percentage change from yesterday's close to today's open. Green = positive gap, red = negative (down-gap).
  • Volume — shares traded so far today.
  • Float — publicly tradeable shares (smaller float = wilder moves).
  • Float Rotation — Volume ÷ Float. Shows how many times the float has been fully traded today. Green ≥ 2× (heavy momentum), yellow 1–2× (moderate), gray < 1× (light). High float rotation on a low-float stock is often the hallmark of a runner.
  • Market Cap, Sector, List Date — additional context. Click any column header to sort ascending/descending.
Links Column (rightmost)
  • Blue chart icon — jumps straight to the Historical tab pre-loaded with that ticker, 5-year range, and ≥ 5% filter so you can instantly see every gap-up this stock has had in the past 5 years.
  • Amber newspaper icon — opens Yahoo Finance News for that ticker in a new browser tab so you can read the catalyst behind today's gap.

Pro tip: Sort by Float Rotation descending first thing in the morning. The top names are turning their entire float multiple times — those are the stocks with the highest crowd interest and the most violent intraday swings. Cross-check the Gap % and Float before sizing a position.

Historical Tab

Per-ticker gap history

Look up every historical gap-up day for any single ticker. Useful for learning how a stock has behaved in the past — did it run or fade after gapping up? Data is fetched from Polygon and cached locally, so the second load is instant.

How to Search
  1. Type a ticker symbol in the input box (e.g. NVDA).
  2. Choose a period: 1 year, 2 years, 3 years, or 5 years. Longer periods give a fuller picture of the stock's gap behavior.
  3. Set a minimum gap %. 5% shows every gap-up day; 25% shows only major gaps. Start at 5% to see all historical data, then filter up.
  4. Click Load Data. First load fetches from the market data API (may take a few seconds). Subsequent loads use the local cache and are near-instant.
Reading the Results
  • Each row is one historical gap-up day. Columns include date, gap %, open, high, low, close, session volume, dollar volume, and premarket high/time/volume.
  • Summary cards at the top show total gap-up days found and the average gap % across all of them for the selected filter.
  • Expand any row to see intraday stats — whether the stock made its high early and faded, or continued to run past the open.
AI Analysis (Accentor)

After data loads, click Analyze with AI. Accentor AI reads all the gap-up days and tells you: how often this stock runs vs fades after gapping, what gap sizes tend to produce the best intraday moves, and what time of day the high is typically set. This is the fastest way to build a stock-specific playbook.

Shortcut: In the Gap-Ups tab, click the blue chart icon next to any ticker. It automatically opens the Historical tab with that ticker pre-filled, period set to 5 years, and minimum gap % set to 5% — so you get the maximum history in one click.

Swing Trading Tab

AI-graded daily picks

Every trading day, the platform runs an AI pipeline that screens a universe of movers, gap-ups, and volume-surge stocks, then has Claude (Anthropic's AI) grade each one and assign a directional bias. The result is a short list of the highest-conviction swing trade candidates for that day.

Understanding Grades & Bias
  • Grade A — high-conviction setup. Strong technicals, clean chart structure, fits the day's market theme.
  • Grade B — moderate conviction. Worth watching; may need confirmation before entry.
  • Grade C — low conviction. Informational only — not recommended for trading.
  • Bias — Bullish, Neutral, or Bearish. BrownBot only auto-trades Grade A or B with a Bullish bias.
Source Breakdown

The pill buttons at the top of the picks list show how many candidates came from each source: Top Movers (stocks with the largest % move today), Gap-Ups (this morning's scanner results), and Volume Surge (unusual volume spikes). Click a source pill to expand and see only those stocks.

Note: Swing picks refresh once per trading day. If you open this tab after hours or on a weekend, you'll see the most recent session's picks. The AI grading considers SMA-10 relationship, market cap, intraday structure, and overall market context — not just the gap or price action alone.

Positions Tab

Live + closed positions

The Positions tab has two permanently visible sections: live broker positions pinned at the top, and your full closed-trade history below. You never need to toggle between views.

Open Positions (top section)
  • Pulls your live positions directly from Alpaca every time you visit this tab. This works whether BrownBot is running or stopped — it always reads from the broker.
  • Columns: Symbol, Qty, Side (long/short), Avg Entry price, Current Price, Unrealized P&L, Unrealized P&L %, Market Value, Broker name.
  • Click the refresh icon in the section header to re-fetch immediately.
  • If you see "No broker configured" — go to My Account → Broker Settings and enter your Alpaca API keys.
  • On mobile, scroll the table horizontally. The Symbol column stays fixed so you always know which row you're reading.
Closed Positions (bottom section)
  • Shows every trade that has been closed, sourced from your trade history database.
  • Use the date range filters to narrow down to a specific period (e.g. this week, this month).
  • P&L is color-coded: green = profitable close, red = loss.
  • The Symbol column is sticky on mobile for easy reading while scrolling right through the data columns.

Important: BrownBot's in-memory active positions (shown in the BrownBot tab) disappear from that tab when the bot is stopped. The Positions tab Open section is the place to check your broker's actual holdings at any time, independent of whether the bot is running.

BrownBot Tab

Yogi tier — autonomous trader

BrownBot is a fully autonomous trading bot. Once started, it finds its own entry candidates, manages risk, and exits positions without any manual action. It trades two styles simultaneously: day trades (gap-up candidates, 9:35–10:30 ET entry window, flattened by EOD) and swing trades (AI-graded picks held for days to weeks).

Starting the Bot
  1. Make sure your Alpaca API keys are saved in My Account → Broker Settings and the connection test passes.
  2. Configure your settings in the Day Trade and Swing Trade config panels (see below).
  3. Click Start BrownBot. Two background loops launch: a scanner (runs every 30 s) and an exit monitor (runs every 2 s).
  4. Click Stop to shut both loops down cleanly. Any open positions remain in your Alpaca account — check the Positions tab to manage them.
Key Configuration Settings
  • Min Gap % / Price range / Volume — scanner filter. Only gap-up stocks that pass all three are considered for day-trade entry.
  • Entry signals (optional, all off by default): Above VWAP, Bullish 1-min candle, Max extension from gap price, Volume surge vs recent bars. Turn on the ones you want for extra confirmation before entry.
  • Position size % — e.g. 5% means each position uses 5% of your account equity.
  • Profit target % / Stop loss % — exit thresholds from average entry price.
  • Breakeven trigger % — once the position moves this % toward target, the stop is automatically moved to your entry price. Positions at breakeven show a BE badge.
  • Max concurrent positions — how many day or swing slots can be open simultaneously.
  • Max daily loss — circuit breaker. If today's realized P&L hits this threshold (negative), the bot stops entering new trades for the rest of the day.
  • EOD flatten time — all day-trade positions are closed at this time ET regardless of P&L.
Active Positions & Logs
  • Active Positions panel shows real-time unrealized P&L for positions BrownBot is currently managing. This list is in-memory — it clears when you stop the bot. After stopping, use the Positions tab to see what remains in your Alpaca account.
  • Logs feed shows the last 100 events color-coded by severity: blue = info (entry/exit actions), yellow = warning, red = error.
Watchlist

Add any ticker manually to the BrownBot Watchlist. Watchlist symbols are treated as swing-trade candidates and bypass the automatic gap-up scanner — useful for stocks you've identified yourself that you want BrownBot to trade even if they didn't appear in today's scan.

Risk reminder: BrownBot places real orders with real money. Always test with paper trading first (set Paper Trading = on in Broker Settings), verify the circuit breaker and stop loss are configured, and monitor the Logs feed for the first few sessions before walking away.

Stats Tab

Performance analytics

A full performance dashboard built from your closed trade history. Use the date range filter at the top to scope all charts and metrics to any time window.

What's on the Page
  • Summary cards — Total Realized P&L, Win Rate %, and total number of closed positions for the selected period.
  • Daily P&L chart — bar or line chart showing each day's net gain/loss. Toggle between bar and line with the buttons above the chart.
  • Cumulative P&L chart — your running account growth (or drawdown) over the selected period.
  • Pie charts — Long vs Short split (which side makes/loses more), Symbol breakdown (which individual stocks contributed most to P&L), and Win/Loss split (visualising your win rate).
  • Extended Stats — deeper metrics: Profit Factor (gross profit ÷ gross loss; above 1.5 is healthy), Expectancy (average dollar gain per trade), consecutive win/loss streaks, average winning trade, average losing trade, and best/worst single trade.
  • Monthly breakdown — P&L grouped by calendar month for a longer-term view of consistency.

How to use it: Start with the date range set to "last 30 days" to see recent performance. Then widen to "all time" to spot seasonal patterns. If your win rate is high but P&L is negative, check the avg win vs avg loss — you may be cutting winners too early and letting losers run.

Trade History Tab

Filled orders log

A raw log of every filled order synced from your broker. Each row is one execution — a buy or a sell fill with the exact price, quantity, and timestamp.

Filtering
  • Use the date range pickers to narrow to a specific period (today, this week, custom).
  • Type in the symbol filter box to show only trades in a specific stock.
  • Click any column header to sort by that field (date, symbol, P&L, etc.).

Note: This tab shows individual fills (buy/sell legs), not net position P&L. For net position-level P&L (how much you made on each round-trip trade), use the Positions tab. For aggregate statistics, use the Stats tab.

Backtest Tab

Strategy simulation

Simulate a trading strategy against historical gap-up data without risking real money. Use it to validate whether a set of entry/exit rules would have been profitable in the past before applying them live.

Setup
  1. Pick a strategy type (Gap Up, Momentum, Mean Reversion, Breakout).
  2. Set the date range for the simulation.
  3. Configure initial capital, position size per trade, stop loss %, and profit target %.
  4. Click Run Backtest. Results appear as an equity curve chart plus a summary of key metrics.
Reading Results
  • Equity curve — portfolio value over time. A smooth upward slope is ideal; large drawdown periods show when the strategy struggled.
  • Total return, win rate, max drawdown, Sharpe ratio — the four numbers that matter most. A strategy with a good Sharpe ratio (> 1.0) and max drawdown you can stomach is worth considering for live testing.

Warning: Past performance does not guarantee future results. Backtests don't account for slippage, partial fills, or news events. Use them as a directional guide, not a guarantee.

AI Chat Tab

Powered by Claude

A direct chat window to Claude (Anthropic's AI). Unlike a generic chatbot, this assistant has knowledge of how this platform works and can help you interpret your own data, plan trades, and understand market concepts.

What to Ask
  • "Why would a stock with a 30% gap and 5× float rotation be a good day-trade candidate?"
  • "Explain profit factor and what a good number looks like."
  • "My win rate is 60% but I'm still losing money — what could be wrong?"
  • "What's the difference between a runner and a fader after a gap-up?"
  • "How should I set my breakeven trigger in BrownBot for a 10% profit target?"

Note: The AI Chat does not have real-time access to live market prices or your personal account. For real-time analysis of specific gap-ups, use the Historical tab's AI Analysis feature instead, which feeds the AI actual gap-up data for a specific stock.

My Account

Broker & subscription

Manage your subscription, profile, and — most importantly — your broker connection. The broker connection is required for BrownBot to trade and for the Positions tab to show live open positions.

Setting Up Alpaca
  1. Go to alpaca.markets and create a free account.
  2. Navigate to your Alpaca dashboard → Paper Trading (or Live Trading) → API Keys → Generate new key.
  3. Copy the API Key ID and Secret Key.
  4. In this platform, open My Account → Broker Settings → Alpaca. Paste both keys and choose Paper or Live mode.
  5. Click Save then Test Connection. A green success message confirms the connection is working.

Start with Paper Trading: Alpaca's paper trading account is free and simulates real order execution with virtual money. Run BrownBot on paper for at least a week before switching to a live account.
