What is Accentor AI Trading Bot?

Accentor is an advanced algorithmic trading platform designed to help traders identify and capitalize on market opportunities. Our AI-powered system analyzes market data in real-time, detects gap-up patterns, and executes trades automatically based on your predefined strategies. Whether you're a seasoned trader or just starting out, Accentor provides the tools you need to make informed trading decisions.

How to Use Each Tab

Gap-Ups Tab

Purpose: Identify stocks that have opened significantly higher than their previous closing price.

How to Use:
  1. Configure Gap Percentage: Set the minimum gap percentage (default: 25%) to filter stocks
  2. View Gap-Up Stocks: The system automatically scans and displays stocks meeting your criteria
  3. Monitor Real-Time: The list updates automatically every 5 minutes during market hours
  4. Take Action: Use the "Add to Entry Bot" feature to track promising stocks
💡 Pro Tip:

Gap-up stocks often experience increased volatility and trading volume, presenting opportunities for quick profits. However, they also carry higher risk, so always use proper risk management.

Historical Tab

Purpose: Access historical market data and analyze past performance to refine your trading strategies.

How to Use:
  1. Select Date Range: Choose the period you want to analyze
  2. Choose Data Type: Select between gap-up data, trade history, or position history
  3. Apply Filters: Filter by ticker symbol, gap percentage, or other criteria
  4. Export Data: Download data in Excel format for external analysis
💡 Pro Tip:

Use historical data to backtest your strategies and identify patterns that lead to successful trades. This can help you optimize your entry and exit parameters.

Entry Bot Tab

Purpose: Configure automated entry strategies and monitor stocks for entry opportunities.

How to Use:
  1. Check Bot Status: Ensure the bot is connected to your trading platform (DAS)
  2. Set Entry Parameters: Define symbol, volume requirements, and entry time
  3. Submit Strategy: Click "Submit" to start tracking the stock
  4. Monitor Progress: Watch the tracking status for real-time updates
  5. Automatic Entry: The bot will enter positions when all conditions are met
⚠️ Important:

The Entry Bot uses market orders at the ask price. Always verify your parameters before submitting, as entries cannot be cancelled once conditions are met.

Exit Bot Tab

Purpose: Manage active positions and configure automated exit strategies with profit targets and stop losses.

How to Use:
  1. Check Bot Status: Verify connection to your trading platform
  2. View Active Positions: See all currently held positions
  3. Set Exit Parameters: Configure profit targets and stop losses for each position
  4. Enable Auto-Exit: Turn on automated exit monitoring
  5. Monitor Performance: Track P&L and exit conditions in real-time
💡 Pro Tip:

Use trailing stop losses to protect profits while allowing for upside potential. The Exit Bot will automatically close positions when your targets are reached.

Trade History Tab

Purpose: Review completed trades and analyze your trading performance over time.

How to Use:
  1. Filter by Date: Select specific time periods to analyze
  2. Sort and Filter: Organize trades by ticker, P&L, or trade type
  3. View Details: Click on individual trades for detailed information
  4. Export Data: Download trade history for external analysis
  5. Analyze Patterns: Identify which strategies work best for you
📊 Analytics:

Use the trade history to calculate your win rate, average profit/loss, and identify your most profitable trading patterns. This data is crucial for strategy optimization.

Positions History Tab

Purpose: Track the lifecycle of positions from entry to exit, including unrealized and realized P&L.

How to Use:
  1. View All Positions: See both open and closed positions
  2. Filter by Status: Separate active positions from closed ones
  3. Track Performance: Monitor unrealized P&L for open positions
  4. Analyze Results: Review realized P&L for closed positions
  5. Export Data: Download position data for portfolio analysis
📈 Portfolio View:

The Positions History provides a comprehensive view of your portfolio performance. Use it to track your overall trading success and identify areas for improvement.

Stats Tab

Purpose: View comprehensive performance statistics and analytics to track your trading success.

How to Use:
  1. View Performance Metrics: See total P&L, win rate, and total positions
  2. Analyze Daily Performance: Review daily P&L trends with interactive charts
  3. Toggle Chart Types: Switch between bar and line charts for different views
  4. Refresh Data: Get the latest statistics with real-time updates
  5. Track Progress: Monitor your trading performance over time
📊 Performance Analytics:

The Stats tab provides key performance indicators including total realized P&L, win rate percentage, and daily performance trends. Use this data to evaluate your trading strategies and identify areas for improvement.

Backtest Tab

Purpose: Test your trading strategies against historical data to validate their effectiveness before live trading.

How to Use:
  1. Select Strategy: Choose from predefined strategies (Gap Up, Momentum, Mean Reversion, Breakout)
  2. Set Date Range: Define the historical period for testing
  3. Configure Parameters: Set initial capital, position size, and stop loss
  4. Run Backtest: Execute the strategy against historical data
  5. Analyze Results: Review performance metrics, risk analysis, and trade history
  6. View Equity Curve: See how your portfolio would have performed over time
🧪 Strategy Validation:

Backtesting allows you to validate your trading strategies without risking real capital. Analyze key metrics like total return, win rate, max drawdown, and Sharpe ratio to optimize your approach before live trading.

AI Chat Tab

Purpose: Get real-time assistance and insights from our AI trading assistant.

How to Use:
  1. Ask Questions: Inquire about market conditions, trading strategies, or platform features
  2. Get Analysis: Request analysis of specific stocks or market trends
  3. Strategy Advice: Get recommendations for entry/exit parameters
  4. Technical Support: Ask for help with platform functionality
  5. Market Insights: Receive real-time market commentary and analysis
🤖 AI Assistant:

Our AI assistant has access to real-time market data and can provide personalized trading insights. Use it to enhance your decision-making process and stay informed about market developments.

Getting Started Guide

Step-by-Step Process for New Users

1
Configure Your Settings

Start by visiting the Gap-Ups tab and setting your preferred minimum gap percentage. This will determine which stocks appear in your scan results.

2
Identify Opportunities

Browse the Gap-Ups tab to find stocks that meet your criteria. Look for stocks with strong volume and clear upward momentum.

3
Set Up Entry Strategy

Go to the Entry Bot tab and configure your entry parameters. Define volume requirements, entry time, and other criteria.

4
Configure Exit Strategy

Visit the Exit Bot tab to set profit targets and stop losses for your positions. This is crucial for risk management.

5
Monitor and Analyze

Use the Trade History and Positions History tabs to track your performance and refine your strategies based on results.

Entry Parameters

DAS Order Parameters

Entry Parameters Guide

Symbol: Stock ticker symbol (e.g., AAPL, TSLA)
Total Volume: Total volume the stock has traded since pre-market open in millions
Dollar Volume: Calculated as Total Volume traded * Current Price of the stock (M)
Entry Time: Time after which the bot should consider entries (e.g., 10:00 AM ET)
Order Side: B for Buy, S for Sell
Route: Trading route (default: SMAT)
Quantity: Number of shares to trade
Order Type: MKT for Market orders, LIMIT for Limit orders
Limit Price: Required for LIMIT orders (optional for MKT orders)

How These Statistics Are Calculated

Total P&L: Sum of all realized profits and losses from the positions table SELECT SUM(REALIZED) AS TOTAL_PNL FROM POSITIONS;
Win Rate: Percentage of winning positions (realized > 0) vs losing positions (realized < 0) (SELECT COUNT(*) FROM POSITIONS WHERE REALIZED > 0) / (SELECT COUNT(*) FROM POSITIONS WHERE REALIZED < 0)
Total Positions: Count of all positions in the positions table SELECT COUNT(*) FROM POSITIONS;
